# Approved Calibration Orders

Approved calibration orders are **authorized requests** used to send gages out for maintenance or calibration. Once approved, the order is considered official and may be sent to the calibration supplier for processing. Calibration orders function similarly to purchase orders and follow an approval workflow.

**Also known as:** *Gage Calibration Orders*, *Approved Calibration Orders*

---

## What Is an Approved Calibration Order?

An approved calibration order indicates that:
- The order has been reviewed and finalized
- The **Approved** checkbox is selected
- The order is ready to be **sent to the calibration supplier**
- The calibration supplier may act on the order and begin calibration work

Only approved calibration orders should be sent to suppliers.

---

## How Approved Calibration Orders Are Created

Approved calibration orders are created using the same steps as unapproved calibration orders, with one key difference: the order is marked as approved before being sent.

### Basic Workflow

1. Navigate to the **Gaging** module from your dashboard.
2. Click **Calibration Orders**.
3. Select **Create New Calibration Order**.
4. Search for gages by:
   - Station
   - Assigned calibrator
   - Percent of calibration life remaining
   - Expiration date
5. Add gages to the order manually or allow the system to check automatically.

---

## Approving the Calibration Order

To approve the order:

1. Ensure all required gages are added.
2. Check the **Approved** checkbox.
3. Click **Save**.

Once approved, the order no longer appears as red or blank and is considered ready for submission.

---

## Sending the Order to the Supplier

After approval:
- You may send the calibration order directly to the supplier.
- The supplier will use the order details to perform calibration or maintenance work.

---

## Important Notes

- Suppliers should **only receive approved calibration orders**.
- Unapproved orders are for internal coordination and review only.
- Approved calibration orders represent a confirmed request and should be accurate and complete before submission.
